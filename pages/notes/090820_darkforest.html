reflections on the dark forest

Some notes on Liu Cixin's The Dark Forest (one of my top favourite books; major spoilers ahead):

  • the primary goal of every civilisation is survival
  • matter in the universe is limited, but the expansion of civilisations is not
  • chain of suspicions

The dark battle; doomsday battle; microcosm of the dark forest theory:

  • teardrop, perfectly mirror surfaced trisolaran probe is sent to the space fleet of earth
  • in 20 odd minutes destroys nearly all of them, except for Natural Selection and Bronze Age; Blue Space?
  • once they all escape they set their course to the nearest moons of Jupiter to refuel
  • but plans after that remain undetermined
  • communications between vessels either slow, inhibited or unavailable: chains of suspicion develop
  • Zhang Beihai plans for the use of hydro-something bombs to attack other vessels and claim their resources to rapidly increase their longevity but is too late as another ship attacks them
  • soon all ships start attacking
  • communication is not to be trusted in the first place due to distance, or the truth simply cannot be confirmed

Beyond distance, ecological, psychological and essentially biological differences between not only humans but also extraterrestrial beings inhibit trust and truths in communication (re: how trisolarans are unable to conceal the truth due to their conscience and voice being one and the same)

The dark forest theory:

  • every civilisation is a hunter stalking through the forest, careful not to leave any traces behind
  • any and all forms of alien life will be eliminated immediately
  • firstly, the reveal of one's location to another is deadly in itself. if a civilisation finds out the general location they only have one option:
    • **important**; if a civilisation has found the location of another, sooner or later the reverse will be true. cognisant of that:
    • if the revealed civilisation is low level; taking into consideration the explosive exponential growth of technology and power, they will one day grow strong in power.
    • if the revealed civilisation is high level; they have no choice but to still attempt an assault either now or in the future for fear of being wiped out

Possibilities under this theory:

  • nobody has found us yet
  • some civilisation has found us and are plotting to eliminate us
  • some civilisation has found us and are in the process of eliminating us (maybe they're lightyears away so it takes centuries/millenia to get here)
  • some civilisation is "grooming us": cutting edge technology allows them to chart our development; monitor it. at our apex, they invade and take control of our resources, ideas and knowledge, before moving on
  • we are still in the very early stages of development; none of this has happened or will happen just yet, but it will happen in the future
  • or... extraterrestrial life simply does not exist.
