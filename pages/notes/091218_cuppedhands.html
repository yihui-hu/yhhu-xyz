cupped hands

Rubbing palms together in between pauses. [Music] continues without missing a beat [clock still ticks]. You never notice how [...] is, or the fact that [...]. Why then, is it only in such situations that these are made all the more apparent? [He looks off to the distance searching for something else to occupy him, but futility forces him to deliver a quick glance before finally lowering his eyes back to his cupped hands.]

The lobby has grown quiet. Only the background bossa nova soundtrack remains, with occasional clicks of the mouse from the receptionist. A middle-aged man with silver hair dressed in casual black, seated at the high table, scrolls through spreadsheets, tallying up today’s count. The placard of the receptionist’s counter now reads “closed”: a polite cue for me to head off to bed. I’m beat.
